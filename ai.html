Carcinization

Carcinization is an example of convergent evolution in which a crustacean evolves into a crab-like form from a non-crab-like form. This phenomenon has occurred independently in several different lineages of decapod crustaceans, making it a fascinating subject of study in evolutionary biology.

Fun Facts About Crabs

  • There are over 6,800 species of crabs.
  • Crabs can walk in all directions, but they usually walk sideways.
  • Some crabs can live up to 100 years.
  • Crabs have a hard exoskeleton called a carapace.
  • Crabs communicate by drumming their pincers or waving their claws.

Crab Habitats

Crabs are found in all of the world's oceans, in fresh water, and on land. They typically prefer shallow coastal waters where they can find plenty of food and shelter. Some crabs, like the coconut crab, have adapted to life on land and can be found in tropical regions.
